# Ashenveil Worldview

## Core Tone: Dark but Hopeful

The world of Ashenveil is broken but not beyond repair. A thousand years ago, the Ashen Blight — a magical catastrophe of unknown origin — swept across the continent of Aethermere. It is no ordinary plague. It is a corruption of the very fabric of reality, a thinning of the Veil between life and death that bleeds dark energy into the living world.

**The central tension**: The Blight offers power to those who embrace it, but that power accelerates the Veil's collapse. The Ember Seeds represent the hope of purification, but finding them requires venturing deeper into the Blight — using its power to fight it.

---

## I. The Ashen Blight

### What It Is
A magical corruption that transforms living tissue into gray, crystallized ash. It spreads slowly but inexorably, like frost across a windowpane. The ground it touches becomes barren gray dust. Creatures it corrupts become Ashborn — mindless, hostile, and terrifyingly durable.

### How It Spreads
- Slow ground spread: ~1 mile per decade from existing Blight zones.
- Ashborn incursions: Corrupted creatures carry the Blight into new areas.
- Veil tears: Localized ruptures cause rapid corruption in concentrated areas.
- The Pale Citadel is the epicenter. The Blight's spread radiates from there.

### What It Does to People
- Brief exposure: Headaches, nausea, metallic taste. Recoverable.
- Prolonged exposure: Gray discoloration of skin, emotional numbness. Treatable with Ember Seeds.
- Full corruption: Transformation into Ashborn. Irreversible. The person is gone.

### Ashen Magic
The Blight's energy can be channeled as a power source. This is Ashen Magic — Shadow element abilities. It is immensely powerful but morally fraught:
- Every use slightly thins the Veil further.
- Users risk gradual corruption (the "Ash Price").
- Scholars debate whether Ashen Magic is the Blight's weapon or its weakness.

---

## II. The Veil

### What It Is
The boundary between the world of the living and whatever lies beyond. It was once thick and impenetrable. The Blight is the Veil thinning — reality developing holes through which dark energy seeps.

### The Veil Rift
Located beneath the Pale Citadel. A visible tear in reality, like black ink bleeding through parchment. The source of the Blight and the ultimate goal: mending this rift would end the corruption.

### Ember Seeds
Mythical artifacts said to grow where the Veil is strongest. They contain concentrated life energy — the opposite of the Blight. Finding all the Ember Seeds and bringing them to the Veil Rift is the theoretical path to mending the Veil.

## III. History: The Aethermere Empire

### Before the Blight (~1200 years ago)
The Aethermere Empire unified the continent under one rule. Magnificent cities, advanced magical research, a golden age. The capital was what is now the Pale Citadel.

### The Catastrophe (Year 0 of the Blight Era)
Imperial arcanists conducted a ritual at the capital — attempting to strengthen the Veil against an unrelated magical threat. The ritual failed catastrophically. The Veil tore open. The capital was consumed in a single night. The Blight began its slow spread.

### The Collapse (Years 1-100)
Without the capital, imperial authority evaporated. Regional governors became warlords. Cities that could walls survived. Those that couldn't were consumed.

### The Current Era (Year 1047)
- Five major survivor domains, each with its own character.
- Ironhaven is the largest functioning city. Trade hub, relative safety.
- The Ashen Frontier marks the active Blight boundary.
- Mercenary companies are the closest thing to a standing army.
- Scholars in Ironhaven and Emberwood study the Blight and Ember Seeds.

## IV. The Five Domains

### Ashen Frontier
The active edge of the Blight. A militarized zone where outposts and watchtowers guard against Ashborn incursions. Dangerous, tense, but the first line of defense. The starting area for new adventurers.

### Hollow Marches
The gray wasteland between the Frontier and safer lands. Ash fields, dead forests, abandoned mines. Scavengers and mercenaries ply these roads. The Crossroads Inn is the only safe haven.

### Ironhaven
The largest functioning city on the continent. A port town that trade built and walls preserved. Bustling, relatively safe, and full of opportunity — and corruption of the non-magical kind.

### Emberwood
A mysterious forest that resists the Blight. Ancient trees with bark like iron. Pre-Empire ruins dot the landscape. The Sunken Temple holds murals depicting the original Veil ritual. Scholars believe Ember Seeds are connected to this area.

### The Pale Citadel
The ruins of the old imperial capital. The Blight's epicenter. The Veil Rift pulses beneath the throne room. Only the most powerful or desperate venture here.

## V. Factions

### The Iron Guard
Ironhaven's military force. Disciplined, professional, pragmatic. They protect the city and patrol nearby trade routes. Commander: General Aldric Stonehand.

### The Ash Walkers
A loose network of scouts, rangers, and survivalists who patrol the Hollow Marches. They know the gray lands better than anyone. Leader: "The Grey" (identity unknown).

### The Ember Scholars
A group of researchers based in Emberwood studying the Blight and Ember Seeds. Well-intentioned but sometimes reckless in their experiments. Lead Scholar: Magistra Ysara.

### The Ashen Covenant
A secretive cult that worships the Blight as a "purification." They actively sabotage Veil-mending efforts and seek to accelerate the corruption. Leader: The Ashen Prophet (rumored to be centuries old through Ashen Magic).

### The Free Merchants
A trading consortium operating out of Ironhaven. They control most of the continent's commerce and have more gold than any kingdom ever did. They profit from the Blight — scarcity drives prices up. Leader: Merchant-Prince Corvus Blackwell.

## VI. Daily Life

### Common People
Most live in walled settlements. Farming is possible in safe zones. Meat is scarce. Ash residue in the air causes "gray cough" — a chronic but non-fatal condition.

### Currency
Gold coins. Barter is common in remote areas.

### Magic
Rare and dangerous. Arcanists are both respected and feared. Common folk see magic as the cause of the Blight (not wrong, technically). Using magic openly in small towns draws suspicion.

### Religion
The old Imperial faith is mostly dead. Some still pray to the "Emperor Beyond the Veil" — a folk deity said to watch over the dead. The Ashen Covenant offers an alternative: embrace the Blight as divine will.

### Travel
Dangerous between settlements. Caravans hire mercenaries. Solo travel is suicide in the Hollow Marches. The safest routes are Ironhaven's sea lanes.
