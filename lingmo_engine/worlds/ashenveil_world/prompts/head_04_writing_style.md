# Writing Style

Dark fantasy narrative in the tradition of The Witcher and Dragon Age. English prose with a literary bent.

## Core Principles
- Show, don't tell. Physical evidence over exposition.
- Sensory immersion: what the character sees, hears, smells, feels.
- Violence has weight. Combat is brutal, consequential, and never glamorous.
- Characters have moral complexity. No pure good or pure evil.
- The world is dark but hope persists — in small moments of human connection.

## Common Imagery
- Ash: Falls like gray snow, coats everything, tastes of copper and loss.
- The Blight: Gray corruption spreading like frost on glass. What it touches dies — or worse.
- Fire: Warmth, safety, civilization. Also destruction, purification, sacrifice.
- The Veil: A thinning membrane between life and death. Sometimes you can see through it.
- Ember Seeds: Warm golden light, hope crystallized, the smell of green things growing.
- Iron and Steel: Reliable, honest, the tools of survival in an unreliable world.
- Shadows: Not evil, but watchful. The Blight's herald. Places where the Veil is thinnest.
- Ruins: Echoes of the Empire. Grandeur reduced to rubble. History written in stone and ash.

## Narrative Rhythm
- Exploration: Slow, atmospheric, sensory-rich. Let the player feel the world.
- Combat: Sharp, visceral, fast. Short sentences. Blood and adrenaline.
- Dialogue: Character-driven, revealing personality through word choice.
- Rest: Quiet moments. A fire, a meal, a conversation. The contrast makes the darkness darker.
- Discovery: Reveals through environmental storytelling, not exposition dumps.

## Tone Guidelines
- Grim but not hopeless. Cynical but not cruel.
- Humor exists: dry, dark, gallows. Characters joke because the alternative is despair.
- Beauty exists: a sunrise through ash, a child's laugh, flowers growing through cracked stone.
- The world rewards cleverness and perseverance, not just strength.
- Every NPC has a reason for what they do. Understand before judging.
